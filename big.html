Is solving this problem an actual challenge?

File CHARTEVENTS.csv.gz contains records of several events of patients collected during their stay in the ICU (Intensive Care Unit). This file, compressed, occupies 4.2 Gigabytes. Documentation about this table and the database source can be found here. You need to perform a full data analysis and learning with this data, using a traditional machine learning pipeline: data preprocessing, data preparation, training and validation, analysis of results and interpretation. You are required to use suitable tool(s) in order to efficiently process this data. Among the tasks you will perform are:
  • statistical analysis and visualization of data for each patient (SUBJECT_ID). Note: one patient can have more than one hospital admission (HADM_ID).

    • An example of visualization graph is shown below. The X axis shows one full day of intervention for patient whose ICUSTAY_ID is 200072. X=0.25 means the first quarter of one day. Each dot corresponds to a different item, whose codes appear on the right hand side bar (for example, item 226000 corresponds to nutrition supplement given to the patient - an input event). The Y axis shows the amount of the item given to the patient. To know more about items, item IDs and their descriptions you need to consult table D_ITEMS.csv.

  • Predict length of stay. For this task you will need to choose a window size to train your data (too many days will delay decisions about patients when the system is deployed, too few days will probably produce a very shortsighted predictor. Choose with care).

    • Use wisely the resources you learned (map-reduce, pyspark, bigquery, multiprocessing, multithreading, pipelines etc) in order to analyze this data. Report total execution time and perform profiling, whenever possible, on your ML phases. Discuss about performance issues.
    More tables can be found in this link.
    You should hand in a report of your work (pdf format) and a commented and annotated notebook.
    Itens that will be evaluated:
    • Choices for your preprocessing: ML methods and preprocessing libraries/platform
    • Choices for your data preparation: train-test-split method, cross-validation, target variable, libraries used and platform
    • Choices for training and validation of models, hyper-parameter search and libraries and platform used
    • Quality of analysis, interpretation and discussion of results
